Strategic Positioning

E-fficient is Find and Drive's first commercial product, developed in partnership with Seriti. It is not the big swing — it is a Steady Eddie: a practical, relationship-first product that earns its place in the motor industry by being genuinely useful, not by trying to reinvent the wheel.

While Kredo offers a comparable solution within the Seriti ecosystem, E-fficient is consumer-facing — a meaningful distinction that makes dealer adoption straightforward without stepping on existing workflows.

The Problem

The finance widget does its job — leads come through consistently. The real issue is lead quality, not volume. Most dealerships are running ad campaigns with little to no optimisation and weak SEO, which means the traffic arriving on their sites is the wrong traffic. That flows through the widget and produces leads that go nowhere. The widget is fine; what's feeding it isn't.

E-fficient is a lead magnet first. The value-added digital marketing services layer — delivered in partnership with an existing, reputable provider — is what turns it from a widget into a customer retention play — better traffic in, better leads out, and dealers who see results and stick around.
The Retention Loop
1
Widget Goes Live
Finance widget goes up on the dealer's site. Leads start coming in immediately — roughly 4% of total site traffic.
2
VAS Cleans Up the Traffic
Digital marketing services, delivered in partnership with an existing and reputable provider, fix the ad campaigns and SEO so the right people are landing on the site in the first place.
3
Lead Quality Improves
Better traffic in means better leads out. Dealers start seeing the difference in the quality of enquiries coming through.
4
Dealers Stay, Relationships Grow
Dealers who see results keep the product. Revenue becomes recurring. The relationship with the network strengthens over time.
Commercial Objective

E-fficient is not the product that takes Find and Drive where it needs to go. The goal with the current sales pipeline is simple: get to a point where E-fficient revenue covers cost of sale and clears the non-current liabilities. Self-sustaining, cash flow positive, and well-positioned in the dealer network.

There is an additional strategic upside worth noting: as volume grows, E-fficient begins to generate meaningful data on South African car buyers — their behaviour, intent, and affordability patterns. This is not the primary objective, but it is a valuable by-product that could inform how Find and Drive develops its broader platform over time.

Virtual Dealership
DealersOnline / Supergroup JV — A new paradigm for automotive retail
Structural Context

Find and Drive's virtual dealership IP moving into the DealersOnline / Supergroup joint venture. The co-founders of Find and Drive will become part of DealersOnline in intrapreneur roles — building the next phase of the product within an enterprise structure, with the distribution and infrastructure advantages that brings.

What This Is Not
The virtual dealership is not an aggregator. It carries aggregator capabilities, but that is not the point. Traditional platforms are built around time-on-site, endless scrolling, and showing as many cars as possible. The decision burden lands squarely on the customer — who might find the cheapest BMW on the market without knowing the selling dealer has a poor reputation, the car carries risk, or the monthly payment is completely out of reach.
The Alternative Approach

Find and Drive's platform is built to bring customers as close to a deal as possible through a structured, guided buying journey — not a catalogue browse.

Audience Acquisition

The business targets 3 of the 4 identified automotive buying audiences through focused paid campaigns, while building the brand at the same time. The brand-building is not a nice-to-have — it is deliberate. As recognition grows over time, the cost of acquiring each lead drops, which makes the whole model more efficient the longer it runs.

Lead Flow — End to End
1
Bring the Right People In
Targeted paid campaigns across search and social drive in-market audiences to the platform. Brand campaigns run alongside to build recognition over time.
2
Walk Them Through the Journey
Customers go through a guided, conversational experience — not a filter browse. Needs analysis and pre-qualification happen naturally as part of the process.
3
Score and Sort the Leads
The platform produces scored, categorised leads — pre-qualified, affordability-aligned, with a clear read on intent. Every lead gets a tier based on quality and readiness.
4
Act Fast on the Best, Nurture the Rest
Top leads are picked up immediately and handled with care right through to conversion. Lower-scoring leads go into a structured follow-up process — they are not written off.

The Industry Problem That Exists

Non-taken-up deals are a persistent reality in automotive retail. A customer applies, gets approved, and then simply does not buy. This happens across the industry at significant scale. The prevailing view is that the root cause is a misalignment between what customers want, what they can actually afford, and what dealers are putting in front of them — but this problem has not been cracked because the underlying data on buyer behaviour and intent has never been properly captured or used.

What we believe — and what we want to validate Our hypothesis is that low deal take-up rates are driven by a combination of factors: price shock or dissatisfaction later in the journey, an “apply for every customer” approach, and an industry competing for the same pool of customers. We believe total cost of ownership should be introduced far earlier in the customer journey to improve transparency, build trust, and help customers make confident purchasing decisions from the outset. Ultimately, the only reason a customer should not take up a deal is if they have decided not to purchase a vehicle at all. As Will puts it, “we should treat every customer like we’re selling to our mothers.” Customers should be engaged immediately, and neither finding the right vehicle nor securing the right finance structure should become barriers to concluding a deal. To properly address drop-off rates while gaining a deeper understanding of the underlying causes, we need to operate at scale with a large pool of high-intent, high-quality customers. Higher application volumes from strong customers will allow us to absorb lower take-up rates where they exist, while giving us the data needed to optimise conversion and customer outcomes over time.
Long-Term Vision

The goal is not just to generate leads. It is to build an automotive commerce platform that makes buying a car in South Africa less frustrating, more transparent, and far more efficient for both the customer and the dealer — using technology to do what good salespeople have always done, just at scale.
